Everyday Life: Past, Present and Experience

Simple present, past simple and present perfect for beginner adult ESL students

Grammar Reminder

We use different tenses to talk about routines, finished past actions, and life experiences.

Simple present

Use it for routines and habits.

I drink coffee every morning.

Past simple

Use it for finished actions in the past.

I cooked dinner last night.

Present perfect

Use it for life experiences or actions connected to now.

I have visited Melbourne.

Teacher tip: Before students start, ask them to find the time words: every day, yesterday, last week, already, never, before.

Activity 2: Match the Sentence to the Tense

Choose the correct tense for each sentence.

Answers

  1. I walk to class every day. — Simple present
  2. She visited her friend last weekend. — Past simple
  3. We have eaten lunch already. — Present perfect
  4. He works in the city. — Simple present
  5. They bought a new car in 2022. — Past simple
  6. I have never tried Thai food. — Present perfect

Speaking Practice

Ask and answer these questions with a partner.

  1. What do you usually do in the morning?
  2. What did you do last weekend?
  3. Have you ever studied another language?
  4. What do you usually eat for breakfast?
  5. Did you cook dinner last night?
  6. Have you ever visited another city in Australia?
